Vortex Operator Theory (VOT)
Formal Core XI–XIV: Rigorous Spectral Core

HTML draft • operator-theoretic core extracted from the VOT research program
Purpose.

This note isolates the first rigorous spectral layer of Vortex Operator Theory. The aim is not yet to prove the Riemann Hypothesis, but to identify a mathematically clean core: a self-adjoint operator generated from a VOT-derived potential, together with a precise statement of the gap that still remains.

1. Spectral Objective

The VOT program produces a local purity functional \(\mathcal P(T,\omega)\), shells, 0-points, and event structures. The next formal question is whether these objects can be linked to a genuine self-adjoint operator. The target is an operator of Schrödinger type:

H_{\mathrm{eff}} = -\frac{d^2}{dT^2} + V(T).

The long-term interpretive goal is that the relevant spectral data of \(H_{\mathrm{eff}}\) should encode the zero structure extracted empirically from the VOT architecture.

2. Hilbert Space and Base Operator

Let \(I=[T_{\min},T_{\max}]\subset \mathbb R\) be a compact interval. We work in the Hilbert space

\mathcal H = L^2(I).

Define the differential operator

H_{\mathrm{eff}}u = -u'' + V(T)u

with Dirichlet boundary conditions

u(T_{\min})=u(T_{\max})=0.
Why this setup?

This is the simplest rigorous starting point. On a finite interval, classical Sturm–Liouville and Kato–Rellich theory give a clean self-adjointness result under very mild assumptions on the potential.

3. Minimal Conditions on the Potential

For the first rigorous layer, assume:

  • \(V \in L^\infty(I)\),
  • \(V(T)\in \mathbb R\) for all \(T\in I\).

Equivalently, \(V\) is a bounded real multiplication operator on \(L^2(I)\).

4. Quadratic Form

Associate to \(H_{\mathrm{eff}}\) the quadratic form

q[u] = \int_I \left(|u'(T)|^2 + V(T)|u(T)|^2\right)\,dT.

Since \(V\in L^\infty(I)\), the form is well-defined on \(H_0^1(I)\). If \(V\ge -C\), then the shifted form

q_C[u] = q[u] + (C+1)\|u\|_{L^2(I)}^2

is positive and closed.

5. Theorem A: Self-Adjointness on a Finite Interval

Theorem A.

Let \(I=[T_{\min},T_{\max}]\) be a compact interval and let \(V\in L^\infty(I)\) be real-valued. Then the operator

H_{\mathrm{eff}} = -\frac{d^2}{dT^2} + V(T)

with Dirichlet boundary conditions is self-adjoint on \(L^2(I)\). Moreover, it has compact resolvent and therefore purely discrete spectrum.

Proof sketch

  • The Dirichlet Laplacian \(H_0=-d^2/dT^2\) on \(I\) is self-adjoint.
  • The multiplication operator by \(V\) is bounded and self-adjoint because \(V\in L^\infty(I)\) and is real-valued.
  • Hence \(H_{\mathrm{eff}}=H_0+V\) is self-adjoint by the bounded perturbation theorem (or Kato–Rellich in this bounded case).
  • On a finite interval, the Dirichlet Laplacian has compact resolvent, and bounded perturbations preserve compact resolvent.
Interpretation.

This is the first genuinely rigorous spectral statement available inside the VOT program: once a bounded real potential \(V(T)\) is defined, the corresponding effective operator is mathematically well-posed.

6. How to Construct the Potential from VOT

The empirical VOT framework produces a two-variable purity functional \(\mathcal P(T,\omega)\). A naïve reduction would define

\omega^*(T)=\arg\max_\omega \mathcal P(T,\omega), \qquad F(T)=\mathcal P(T,\omega^*(T)),

and then set

V(T)=-F(T).

However, this direct maximizer can fail to be unique or continuous. To avoid this, a smoothed reduction is preferable.

7. Smoothed Reduction via Log-Sum-Exp

Let \(\Omega\subset\mathbb R\) be a compact frequency window and assume \(\mathcal P\in C^2(I\times\Omega)\). For \(\beta>0\), define

F_\beta(T) = \frac{1}{\beta} \log\int_\Omega e^{\beta \mathcal P(T,\omega)}\,d\omega.

Then define the effective VOT potential by

V_\beta(T)=-F_\beta(T).

8. Theorem B: Regularity of the Smoothed Potential

Theorem B.

Let \(I,\Omega\) be compact intervals and let \(\mathcal P\in C^2(I\times\Omega)\) be real-valued. Then

F_\beta(T)=\frac{1}{\beta}\log\int_\Omega e^{\beta \mathcal P(T,\omega)}\,d\omega

belongs to \(C^2(I)\). Consequently,

V_\beta(T)=-F_\beta(T)\in C^2(I)\subset L^\infty(I),

and the operator

H_\beta = -\frac{d^2}{dT^2}+V_\beta(T)

is self-adjoint on \(L^2(I)\) with Dirichlet boundary conditions and has purely discrete spectrum.

Proof sketch

  • The map \((T,\omega)\mapsto e^{\beta \mathcal P(T,\omega)}\) is \(C^2\) on the compact set \(I\times\Omega\).
  • Differentiation under the integral sign is justified.
  • The logarithm is smooth on positive numbers, and the integral is strictly positive.
  • Hence \(F_\beta\in C^2(I)\), so \(V_\beta\) is bounded and real-valued.
  • Apply Theorem A.

9. Spectral Localization Heuristic

Once \(V_\beta\) is well-defined, one may study the relation between its minima and the low-lying eigenfunctions of \(H_\beta\). If \(F_\beta\) has sharp local maxima at times \(T_1,\dots,T_m\), then \(V_\beta\) has corresponding local minima near those points.

Localization Principle (heuristic).

If \(V_\beta\) has separated nondegenerate local minima, then the low-lying eigenfunctions of \(H_\beta\) localize near those minima.

In VOT language, this suggests the first principled spectral reading:

  • 0-points of the purity geometry create local maxima of \(F_\beta\),
  • these become local minima of \(V_\beta\),
  • the corresponding operator develops localized spectral structure near those regions.

10. Relation to the Original Operator Ansatz

The older VOT layer used the arithmetic field directly in a first-order operator:

H = -i\frac{d}{dt} + \frac12 + A_0(t).

The present rigorous spectral core should be viewed as an effective reduction:

A_0 \;\Longrightarrow\; \mathcal P(T,\omega) \;\Longrightarrow\; F_\beta(T) \;\Longrightarrow\; V_\beta(T) \;\Longrightarrow\; H_\beta.
Interpretive bridge.

The first-order operator \(H\) belongs to the original programmatic layer. The second-order effective operator \(H_\beta\) belongs to the current rigorous spectral layer. The two are not yet proven equivalent. The present strategy is to formalize \(H_\beta\) first, and only afterwards attempt to reconstruct it from the original arithmetic operator framework.

11. The Current Mathematical Gap

Decisive unresolved point.

The VOT program does not yet prove that the spectrum of \(H_\beta\) reproduces the imaginary parts \(\gamma_n\) of the nontrivial zeta zeros. Nor does it yet prove that the effective potential \(V_\beta\) constructed from the purity surface is canonically forced by the arithmetic data.

Thus the present state is:

  • rigorous: construction of a self-adjoint effective operator from a smooth bounded VOT potential;
  • heuristic: interpretation of its spectral minima/localization as zero-generating structures;
  • open: exact spectral identification with \(\{\gamma_n\}\).

12. Strongest Current Theorem Candidate

Core Theorem Candidate.

Let \(I,\Omega\) be compact intervals and let \(\mathcal P\in C^2(I\times\Omega)\) be real-valued. Define

F_\beta(T)=\frac{1}{\beta}\log\int_\Omega e^{\beta \mathcal P(T,\omega)}\,d\omega, \qquad V_\beta(T)=-F_\beta(T),

and

H_\beta=-\frac{d^2}{dT^2}+V_\beta(T)

on \(L^2(I)\) with Dirichlet boundary conditions. Then \(H_\beta\) is a self-adjoint operator with purely discrete spectrum.

This theorem does not solve RH, but it gives a precise and rigorous operator-theoretic nucleus inside the VOT program.

13. Next Rigorous Steps

  1. Prove a localization theorem in the language of VOT shells and 0-points.
  2. Show that the minima structure of \(V_\beta\) is stable under admissible perturbations of \(\mathcal P\).
  3. Formulate a spectral approximation statement of the form
    \lambda_n(H_\beta)\approx \gamma_n.
  4. Attempt a reconstruction link from the original operator
    H = -i\frac{d}{dt}+\frac12+A_0(t)
    to the effective operator \(H_\beta\).

14. Conclusion

The rigorous spectral core of VOT is now clear at the first level: the program can canonically produce a self-adjoint effective operator from a smooth bounded purity-derived potential. This is the first nontrivial formal core that survives independently of the more heuristic empirical architecture.

The central unsolved problem remains the true RH step:

\mathrm{spec}(H_\beta) \stackrel{?}{=} \{\gamma_n\}.

Until that step is proved, VOT remains a serious but incomplete research program rather than a proof.
