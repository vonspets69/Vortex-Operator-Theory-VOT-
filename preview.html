Vortex Operator Theory (VOT): A Dynamic Spectral Framework for the Riemann Hypothesis

Author: Matti S. Kärki
Status: Conceptual preprint / research program
Abstract

This preprint proposes a dynamic spectral interpretation of the Riemann hypothesis. The main idea is that the nontrivial zeros of the zeta function may be understood not merely as a static set of complex numbers, but as resonant spectral values generated by a self-adjoint operator driven by prime-encoded input. The framework introduces a prime-source distribution $K(t)$, a dynamic zero-point field $A_0(t)$, and an operator $$H = -i\frac{d}{dt} + \frac12 + A_0(t).$$ The central claim is not that a proof has already been obtained, but that the Riemann hypothesis may be reformulated as a spectral-dynamic stability problem. Within this framework, the average location of the zeros is approximated by a Lambert-$W$ inversion of the Riemann–von Mangoldt main term, and the oscillatory correction is conjecturally encoded by the field $A_0(t)$ through the relation $A_0(T)\sim S(T)$. The decisive open step is to justify this identification rigorously and to derive the required trace identity from the operator model.

1. Introduction

The Hilbert–Pólya viewpoint suggests that the nontrivial zeros of the Riemann zeta function may arise as the spectrum of a self-adjoint operator. Vortex Operator Theory (VOT) follows this broad direction, but introduces an explicitly dynamic mechanism: the primes act as impulsive input, a damped oscillatory field reacts to that input, and the resulting field deforms an operator whose spectrum is intended to reproduce the imaginary parts of the nontrivial zeta zeros.

The proposal can be summarized schematically as

$$\text{alkuluvut} \;\to\; K(t) \;\to\; A_0(t) \;\to\; H \;\to\; \text{spektri} \approx \{\gamma_n\}.$$

The present document should be read as a structured research program rather than a completed proof. Its purpose is to define the framework clearly, separate established ingredients from conjectural ones, and identify the exact mathematical bottleneck.

2. Operator framework

Work in the Hilbert space

$$\mathcal H = L^2(\mathbb{R}/L\mathbb{Z}),$$

and define the operator

$$H = -i\frac{d}{dt} + \frac12 + A_0(t).$$

Here $-i\,d/dt$ acts as a free scaling generator, the constant term $1/2$ encodes the critical line, and $A_0(t)$ is a real-valued dynamic field. If $A_0(t)\in\mathbb{R}$ and is bounded, then $H$ is self-adjoint in the compact circle model.

3. Prime input as a source distribution

The arithmetic input is represented by the von Mangoldt-type source

$$K(t)=\sum_{p,k}\log p\,\delta(t-k\log p).$$

This encodes not only primes but prime powers as well. In the VOT viewpoint, the role of $K(t)$ is to inject arithmetic structure into a time-domain response system.

4. Dynamic zero-point field

The field $A_0(t)$ is introduced through the response equation

$$\frac{dA_0}{dt}=-\alpha A_0+\beta\sin(\omega t)K(t).$$

Its causal solution is written as

$$A_0(t)=\beta\sum_{p,k:\,k\log p\le t}\log p\,e^{-\alpha(t-k\log p)}\sin(\omega k\log p).$$

This makes the field causal, damped, oscillatory, and explicitly prime-linked. Conceptually, $A_0(t)$ is meant to capture the oscillatory arithmetic correction missing from the mean asymptotic law for the zeros.

5. Main-term approximation for the zeros

The mean position of the nontrivial zeros is approximated by

$$\gamma_n^{(0)}=\frac{2\pi (n-11/8)}{W\!\left((n-11/8)/e\right)}.$$

This is a Lambert-$W$ inversion of the main term of the Riemann–von Mangoldt formula. Numerically, it provides a strong first approximation to the zero ordinates. In earlier tests on the first 1000 zeros, the mean percentage error was approximately 0.08%.

6. Oscillatory correction term

Write the exact ordinate as

$$\gamma_n=\gamma_n^{(0)}+\delta_n.$$

A linearized correction yields

$$\delta_n\approx -\frac{2\pi}{\log(\gamma_n^{(0)}/2\pi)}\,S\!\left(\gamma_n^{(0)}\right),$$

where $S(T)$ denotes the oscillatory part of the argument of the zeta function. This suggests that the true fine structure of the zeros is controlled by an oscillatory term beyond the average law.

7. Central conjectural identification

The key hypothesis of the framework is

$$A_0(T)\sim S(T).$$

If the dynamic zero-point field reconstructs the same oscillatory structure as $S(T)$, then the zero ordinates decompose naturally into a main asymptotic term and a dynamic correction. This is the conceptual bridge between the operator dynamics and the known oscillatory behavior of the zeta zero counting function.

8. Spectral interpretation

The intended spectral mechanism is the following. If one can show that

$$\mathrm{Tr}(e^{-itH})=\sum_{p,k}\log p\,\delta(t-k\log p),$$

then its Laplace transform yields

$$-\frac{\zeta'(s)}{\zeta(s)},$$

and formally one is led to

$$\det(H-E)\propto \xi\!\left(\frac12+iE\right).$$

Under this chain of implications, the spectrum of $H$ would correspond to the nontrivial zeros of the zeta function.

9. Status of the program

  • Strength: the framework unifies prime input, dynamic response, and spectral interpretation in a single model.
  • Numerical motivation: the Lambert-$W$ main-term approximation captures the first 1000 zeros with good accuracy.
  • Critical gap: the theory still requires a rigorous proof that $A_0(t)$ corresponds to $S(T)$ and that the trace identity follows from the operator construction.

10. Mathematical bottleneck

The decisive question is not whether the heuristic picture is suggestive, but whether the operator model can be placed on firm analytic ground. In practical terms, the bottleneck is twofold:

  • to establish a rigorous identification between the constructed field $A_0(t)$ and the oscillatory term $S(T)$;
  • to derive a valid trace formula for $H$ whose transform reproduces the logarithmic derivative of the zeta function.

Without these steps, the framework remains an interesting and potentially fertile research direction, but not a proof.

11. Conclusion

VOT proposes that the Riemann hypothesis may be reformulated as a spectral-dynamic stability problem. In this picture, arithmetic information enters through a prime-source distribution, generates a dynamic zero-point field, and modifies a self-adjoint operator whose spectrum is conjecturally linked to the nontrivial zeta zeros. The proposal is mathematically incomplete, but it isolates a concrete research target: construct the operator rigorously, recover the oscillatory correction term, and prove the trace identity.

Author's note. This document is intended as a preprint-style presentation of a research program. It is not claimed here that the Riemann hypothesis has been proved. The aim is to present the model in a form suitable for mathematical evaluation, criticism, and further refinement.
